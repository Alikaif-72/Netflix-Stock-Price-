Key Insights
📉
COVID-19 Crash & Recovery (Mar 2020)
Volatility spiked to 0.08–0.10 during March 2020 — the highest in the dataset. Netflix recovered quickly as streaming demand surged during lockdowns, making it one of the few stocks to benefit from the pandemic.
⚡
Near-Perfect Bullish/Bearish Balance
With 49.8% Bullish vs 50.1% Bearish days, the stock shows no dominant short-term direction. This is typical of a growth stock — despite even daily splits, the long-term price tripled.
🚀
2021: Peak Performance Year
The average close hit $558 in 2021, the highest of all years. The stock reached its all-time high of $691.69. Post-pandemic streaming boom drove subscriber growth to record highs globally.
📉
Late 2021 – Early 2022 Correction
From the Nov 2021 ATH of ~$691, the stock dropped to $387 by early Feb 2022 — a ~44% correction. Subscriber growth concerns and rising interest rates triggered a sharp Bearish reversal.
📊
MA-7 vs MA-30 Crossovers
When the 7-day moving average crosses above the 30-day, it signals a potential uptrend (Golden Cross). Crossovers in early 2019 and mid-2020 correctly predicted multi-month rallies.
📦
Volume Declining Over Time
Trading volume dropped from ~18–32M shares in 2018 to 2–5M by 2021. This reflects increased institutional holding and reduced retail speculation — hallmark of a maturing, high-price-per-share stock.
💡

Beginner-Friendly Recommendations

1
Use MA crossovers as entry signalsWhen the 7-day MA rises above the 30-day MA, it's often a good time to consider buying. Wait for both to confirm the trend before acting.
2
Avoid high-volatility periodsWhen volatility exceeds 0.05, the stock is unpredictable. Beginners should wait for it to stabilize (below 0.03) before entering positions.
3
Watch trading volume for confirmationPrice moves with high volume are more reliable. A price increase on low volume may not be sustainable — always check volume alongside price.
4
Never time the bottom — buy in phasesThe −22% worst-day shows sharp drops can happen suddenly. Instead of buying all at once, use Dollar Cost Averaging — buy small amounts at regular intervals.
5
Don't panic at Bearish streaks50.1% of days were Bearish — but the overall price still grew ~3× over 4 years. Short-term drops are normal. Focus on the long-term trend, not daily noise.
6
Set stop-loss levels on High-Risk daysWhen the risk_level column shows "High Risk", tighten stop-losses to protect capital. High-risk periods often precede sharp corrections like the Feb 2022 selloff.
